Homemade Pasta Homemade Gnocchi
  1. 2 cups all-purpose flour, spooned & leveled
  2. 3 large eggs
  3. ½ teaspoon sea salt
  4. ½ tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil

    5. Method:

    • Place the flour on a clean work surface and make a nest.
    • Add the eggs, olive oil, and salt to the center and use a fork to gently break up the eggs, keeping the flour walls intact as best as you can.
    • Knead the dough for 8 to 10 minutes and let rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.
    • Dust 2 large baking sheets with flour and set aside.
    • Slice the dough into four pieces. Gently flatten one into an oval disk.
    • Run the dough through the Pasta Roller Attachment three times on level 1 (the widest setting).
    • Set the dough piece onto a countertop or work surface.
    • Fold both short ends in to meet in the center, then fold the dough in half to form a rectangle.
    • Run the dough through the pasta roller three times on level 2, three times on level 3, and one time each on levels 4, 5, and 6.
    • Lay half of the pasta sheet onto the floured baking sheet and sprinkle with flour before folding the other half on top.
    • Sprinkle more flour on top of the second half. Every side should be floured so that your final pasta noodles won't stick together.
    • Repeat with remaining dough
    • Run the pasta sheets through the Pasta Cutter Attachment (pictured is the fettuccine cutter).
    • Cook the pasta in a pot of salted boiling water for 1 to 2 minutes.
  1. Russet Potatoes
  2. 1 egg
  3. Tipo 00 Flour
  4. Semolina flour, for dusting
  5. pinch of salt
  6. Potato Ricer

    7. Method:

    • Cook The Potatoes until tender. (about 25 mins. you want to be able to easily poke a fork into potato.)
    • Once cool enough to handle, but still warm (not hot), remove the skin.
    • Rice the potatoes directly over a large, wide bowl with a light layer of flour.
    • Add egg and pinch of salt.
    • Mix the dough. (Gently bring the dough together to form a cohesive ball)
    • Cut the dough ball into 4 equal pieces.
    • Roll one piece into a 10-12 inch (25-30 cm) log.
    • Cut across into 20-22 gnocchi, about 1/2-inch (1.25 cm) in size to create little “pillows.”
    • Transfer to a sheet pan dusted in semolina flour to prevent sticking. Repeat with the remaining pieces.
    • If you’re going to cook the gnocchi now, have your sauce simmering and ready to go. They take about 1 minute to cook, tops.
    • ** When they float to the top, they’re ready.
Homemade Ravioli
  1. 400 g 00’ Flour (3 cups spooned and levelled)
  2. 4 large eggs free range
  3. 1 egg yolk free range
  4. ½ teaspoon sea salt

    5. Equipment:

    • Pasta Wheel
    • Ravioli Stamp
    • Ravioli Maker

    Method:

    • Place the flour on a clean work surface and make a nest.
    • Add the eggs, olive oil, and salt to the center and use a fork to gently break up the eggs, keeping the flour walls intact as best as you can.
    • Knead the dough for 8 to 10 minutes and let rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.
    • Dust 2 large baking sheets with flour and set aside.
    • Slice the dough into four pieces. Gently flatten one into an oval disk.
    • Run the dough through the Pasta Roller Attachment three times on level 1 (the widest setting).
    • One important tip when using a ravioli maker is to dust it lightly with semolina or 00 flour to stop the pasta sticking to it .
    • Place your sheet of pasta directly over the ravioli maker making sure there is enough overlap on all of the edges.
    • Place the plastic dimples over the sheet of pasta and very lightly press to make little pockets for your filling.
    • Fill the pockets with your desired filling (1 heaped teaspoon).
    • Next, place your second sheet of pasta directly over the first and lightly push down to get rid of any air.
    • Use your mini roller to roll over the ravioli maker until the pasta is cut around the edges.
    • ARemove the excess pasta dough from around the ravioli maker and turn it over to remove the ravioli.
    • Top Tip: If your pasta has trouble sticking together then you can dab it very lightly with water to seal.
