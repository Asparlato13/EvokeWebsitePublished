Desserts

  1. 2 tbsp room temp water
  2. 2 tbsp melted coconut oil
  3. 1 tsp vanilla extract
  4. 1/2 cup coconut sugar
  5. 1/4 tsp sea salt
  6. 1/4 cup Protein Powder
  7. 1/2 cup flour (GF, buckwheat or plain)
  8. 1/4 tsp baking soda
  9. 1/4 cup vegan choc chips
  10. Method:
    • Preheat the oven to 160*C. Line a tray with non-stick baking paper and set aside.
    • In a small bowl, combine the water, coconut oil, vanilla, coconut sugar and see salt and mix well.
    • Add in the protein, buckwheat flour and baking soda and mix until it forms a cookie dough. Fold through the chocolate chips
    • Transfer the dough into the fridge for 30 minutes to harden up slightly.
    • Roll into 9 small cookie dough balls and evenly space out on the tray. Add an extra tiny pinch of choc chips to each and flatten slightly.
    • Bake in the oven for 10-12 minutes or until golden. Cool at room temperature slightly before enjoying (fresh cookies out of the oven come out very soft).
  1. 1 packed cup desiccated coconut
  2. 1/2 packed cup blanched almond flour
  3. 1/2 tbsp Dragon Fruit (Pink Pitaya) Powder
  4. 1/3 cup rice malt syrup
  5. 2 tbsp chilled coconut cream
  6. 1/2 tsp vanilla
  7. Method:
    • Add the coconut, almond flour and dragon fruit powder into a bowl. Mix well to combine.
    • Add in the remaining ingredients and mix well until you reach a mixture that resembles wet sand. If it’s too dry, add in more coconut cream and if it’s too wet, add in more desiccated coconut.
    • Roll into nine balls then coat in more coconut.
    • Enjoy immediately and keep any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge.
  1. 1 cup non-dairy milk
  2. 1/3 cup neutral oil
  3. 1/2 tbsp vanilla extract
  4. 1/2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  5. 2 cups plain flour
  6. 2/3 cup coconut sugar
  7. 1/3 cup Authentic Chai Powder, plus extra to sprinkle on top
  8. pinch of salt
  9. 3 tsp baking powder
  10. 3/4 cup vegan choc chips
  11. Method:
    • Pre-heat the oven to 180°C. In a large mixing bowl combine the milk, oil, vanilla and vinegar. Whisk well.
    • Add the flour, sugar, Authentic Chai, baking powder and sea salt. Mix until smooth.
    • Fold through the chocolate chips. Transfer into a line to loaf pan, top with extra chocolate chips and Authentic Chai and bake in the oven for 15 minutes or until cooked through.
    • Cool on a wire rack before slicing. Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for 2-3 days or in the fridge for 1 week.
