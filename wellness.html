Journaling

Journaling is a powerful tool for connecting with your inner self and finding alignment. It offers a valuable opportunity to contemplate daily experiences, relationships, and personal values, facilitating a deeper understanding of your thoughts and emotions. Emotions act as signals, carrying important messages that guide us towards what aligns with us and away from what no longer serves us. Engaging in thoughtful reflection not only strengthens your self-relationship but also enhances the connections you establish with others. While regular journaling tends to yield the most benefits, it is not essential to write every single day. To kickstart your journaling practice, here are some prompts for inspiration:

Questions to Ask Yourself:

Morning
  • What am I grateful for?
  • What is my intention?
  • How do I want to move throughout the day?
  • How do I feel today?
  • What am I happy about?
    • What about that makes me happy?
  • What am I excited about?
    • What about that makes me excited?
  • What am I proud about?
  • What am I committed to?
  • What really matters to me?
Evening
  • What have I learned today?
  • What did I enjoy?
  • What did I contribute or improve?
  • In what ways have I been a giver?
  • How has today added to the quality of my life?
  • How can I use today as an investment into my future?
  • What interest did I explore today?
Self-reflection
  • What values do I consider most important in life? How do I actions align with those values?
  • What three changes can I make to live according to my personal values?
  • What do I appreciate most about my personality? What aspects do I find harder to accept?
  • List three personal beliefs that I am willing to reconsider or further explore.
  • What difficult thoughts or emotions come up most frequently for me?
  • Which emotions do I find hardest to accept (guilt, anger, disappointment, etc.)? How do I handle these emotions?
  • What parts of daily life cause stress, frustration, or sadness?
  • What can I do to change those experiences?
  • What are three self-defeating thoughts that show up in my self-talk?
  • How can I reframe self-defeating thoughts to encourage myself instead?
  • What go-to coping strategies help me get through moments of emotional or physical pain?
  • What do I fear most?
  • Have my fears changed throughout life?
Monthly
  • What went well?
  • What did not go well?
  • What did I learn from this month's experience?
  • How can I be more mindful next month?
  • What progess have I made fro last month to now that I can celebrate?
  • What empowers me to grow?
  • When was the last time I treated myself?
Relationships
  • What do I value most in relationships (trust, respect, sense of humor, etc.)?
  • How do I draw strength from loved ones?
  • How does this person make me feel? (Am I drained or energized?)
  • What three important things have I learned from previous relationships?
  • What five traits do I value most in potential partners?
  • What are three things working well in my current relationship?
  • What are three things that could be better?
  • What boundaries could I set in your relationships to safeguard your own well-being?
  • What does love mean to me?
  • How do I recognize it in a relationship?
  • What has to happen in order to feel love?
Career
  • How does work fulfill you?
  • Does it leave me wanting more?
  • What part of my workday do I most enjoy?
  • What about my work feels real, necessary, or important to me?
  • What are my career ambitions?
  • What can I do to improve my work performance?
  • What tasks are fun?
  • How often do you find myself in flow state and what am I doing?
