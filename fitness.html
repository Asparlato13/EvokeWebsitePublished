How I lost 50+ lbs!

  1. Focus
    • Eliminating everything that does not align with nor aid to acheiving the main goal (to be healthy).
  2. Movement
    • Non-negotionable 60 mins of daily movement.
    • Yoga, walking, weight lifting, jump roping, golf, and pickleball are my go-to exercises.
  3. Nutrition
    • Garbage in, garbage out.
    • You cannot 'out run' a bad diet.
  4. Listening to my body
    • Find what feels good, each day may look different. Gage where you're at today and make adjustments from there.
    • Meeting my appropriate edge.
    • Prioritizing rest and recovery.
  5. Time
    • It is a life style change.
    • Diets and temporary effort will work long-term.
    • Results will not come over night.
  6. Consistency
    • Showing up is the hardest part.
    • Motivation is unreliable, it comes and goes. Discipline is the key to success.
  7. Tracking progress
    • * You cannot manage what you do not measure.
    • Track your moods, physique, foods, what went well, what did not go well, and what you will improve.

Why You Should Start Walking More:

  1. Increases cardiovascular and pulmonary (heart and lung) fitness
  2. Stronger bones and improved balance
  3. Increases muscle strength and endurance
  4. Reduces body fat and tones your legs
  5. Reduces risk of heart disease and stroke
  6. Boosts immune function
  7. Eases joint pain
  8. It is free
  9. Requires no equipment
  10. Can be done any where, any time, with anyone
  11. Improves your mood
  12. May help clear your head and help you think creatively
  13. Lowers stress
  14. Delays the onset of varicose veins
  15. Stimulates your digestive system
  16. Improves your sleep
  17. Extends your life
  18. A theraputic nature bath

*Your Goal:

  • Walk for 30 mins daily.
  • Work up to 10,000 steps a day.

How to Make Walking an Everday Habit:

  • Park further away when running errands
  • Take the stairs instead of the elevator
  • Walk around the house while your coffee brews
  • Take a "walking meeting" instead of sitting at your desk
  • Burn calories and maintain a healthy weight
  • Get off public transport one stop earlier and walk to work or home
  • Walk (do not drive) to the local shops
  • Making walking a pleasure by
    • walking with friends
    • varying where and when you walk
    • walking the dog
    • Invest in an at-home treadmill

Want more information? Check out these sources:

  • Why Walking is The Most Underrated Form of Exercise (NBC)
  • 5 Surprising Benefits of Walking (Harvard.edu)
  • Walking for Good Health (BetterHealth.gov)
